## Two Tiers: Bread Box vs. Loafbox

LoafHosts keeps it simple. You pick a tier, pick your game, pick a location, and you're live in about 60 seconds.

**Bread Box (BB)** runs on AMD Ryzen 5950X hardware with DDR4 RAM. It's the budget-friendly option — starts at $5/month, goes up to $100/month depending on RAM. Good for small-to-medium communities, private servers, casual modpacks.

**Loafbox** steps up to AMD 7950X3D or 9950X chips (4.8–6.0 GHz, the kind of clocks that make Minecraft chunk loading feel like nothing), DDR5 RAM, and NVMe SSD storage. Starts at $10/month, tops out at $175/month for 64GB RAM. This is for large servers, heavy modpacks, or anyone who's ever rage-quit because their server lagged during a raid.

Both tiers include: unlimited player slots (no per-player fees, ever), Path.net DDoS protection, the LHyper control panel, full mod support, and 24/5 expert support. The Loafbox tier also adds priority support, 50GB org storage, and access to Loafboards.com — a stats leaderboard system for Arma Reforger with kills, deaths, K/D, rank progression, badges, and ban evader detection.

## The LHyper Panel

Most hosting providers give you a reskinned Pterodactyl panel and call it a day. LoafHosts built their own — LHyper — and it's genuinely different.

The headline features: a Server Splitter (run multiple game servers from one plan), a game-switcher, live console with real-time CPU/RAM/disk graphs, a visual config editor, firewall manager, and a Recycle Bin for accidentally deleted files. The Minecraft-specific tools pull modpacks from six sources (CurseForge, Modrinth, ATLauncher, Feed The Beast, Technic, Voids Wrath) and plugins from five. Arma Reforger gets Workshop mod management and Discord webhook integration that auto-posts your full modlist.

It's the kind of control panel where you don't need to Google anything twice.
